Noor Oriental Rugs: Three Centuries of Weaving Artistry

For over three hundred years, the Nooraee family has transmitted the precious heritage of weaving Persian rugs from generation to generation. Today, founder, Harvard Law alumnus, and president Mo Nooraee continues that legacy as a master weaver and one of the nation's foremost certified appraisers and restorers of fine hand-knotted rugs.

Giving Back

Community Impact: Giving Back

Service is the foundation of our work ethic. We are proud to facilitate rug donations that benefit local and global communities.

"The Gems and Treasures are Service"

Born into the seventh generation of a family of Persian weavers in Isfahan, Mo Nooraee came under the tutelage of a local master weaver at the age of seven. For his first lesson, instead of seating him at a loom, the teacher had Mo search the workshop for its hidden gems and treasures. Finding nothing, Mo returned discouraged. The master weaver told him: "The gems and treasures are service, service, service, Mo."

Since its establishment in 1979, Noor Oriental Rugs has been committed to community service. We have donated hundreds of thousands of dollars’ worth of woven treasures to charitable causes, including schools, hospitals, research organizations, cultural institutions, and outreach to the homeless.

Donation Guide & Policies

Official Rug Donation Guidelines

Please review our procedures, tax policies, and community initiatives below.

Director: Mohammad Nooraee serves as the Director of the Sufi Service Committee (Boston), a charitable organization formed under California Laws to address the most pressing needs of our local community.

Outreach Initiatives: The Sufi Service Committee actively sponsors weekly shelter brunches, coat/clothing drives, and food bank transport throughout the Cambridge and Somerville areas. Our monthly progress and financial reports are publicly accessible at www.nimatullahisufiboston.org/charity.

Depending on the condition, appraised value, and the recipient's capability to process textile donations, Noor arranges rug giving through four distinct methods:

  • A. Qualified 501(c)(3) Charities: Noor coordinates direct delivery to verified non-profits (such as YMCA, Rosie's Place, St. Cecilia’s, and Somerville Housing Coalition) with zero delivery charge to the donor. The charity may keep the rug for use or auction it off during a fundraising drive.
  • B. Direct Outreach: Less expensive rugs are presented directly to homeless individuals or low-income families in need of floor coverings. Noor secures direct receipts and provides copies to donors. (Note: These direct outreach donations are not tax-deductible).
  • C. Sale and Forwarding Proceeds: Since keeping rugs at Noor incurs active insurance and maintenance costs ($50/month per rug), rugs that cannot be held long-term or those rejected by charities due to lack of space are sold by Noor. 100% of net proceeds are then forwarded directly to the charity on the donor's behalf.
  • D. Consignment from Charity: Noor delivers the rug to the charity and obtains their signature on IRS Form 8283 for the donor's tax deduction. The charity then consigns the rug back to Noor to manage the sale. Subsequent sale is an independent contract between Noor and the organization, separating the donor from further involvement.

Tax Form 8283: For tax-deductible donations to IRS Section 170(c) qualified organizations, Noor manages the documentation. We legibly complete Section I (description), Section II (declaration of appraiser, if fee is paid), and Section IV (recipient acknowledgment) and secure the authorized charity signature before mailing the form to the donor in time for calendar year tax filing.

Appraisal Thresholds:

  • Rugs under $500: A certified appraisal is not required by the IRS.
  • Rugs over $500: A certified appraiser must fill out Section III of Form 8283. The donor is responsible for the appraisal fee.

Timeline: Due to the necessary cleaning, restoration, and non-profit matching process, rug donations typically take at least one year to fully execute. Noor does not provide legal or tax advice, and donors are encouraged to consult their tax specialists.

Why Donate Instead of Sell? Selling high-value rugs requires expert appraisal, transport, a physical showroom, and considerable time. Consignment has no guaranteed timeframe, and listing rugs publicly (e.g. on Craigslist) introduces privacy and home security risks.

How Noor Simplifies the Process: We manage the entire lifecycle of the donation: pick-up logistics, certified appraisal, professional restoration, and delivery to a verified charity.

  • Free Pick-Up: Noor provides a free collection service in our company van for local donors and utilizes UPS ground services nationwide for remote donors.
  • Mandatory Wash & Restoration: Noor washes and restores all rugs prior to donation so they are in acceptable condition for recipient charities. The service cost is typically covered by the donor, directly increasing the rug's appraised value.
  • Appraisal Rates: Certified appraisals by Harvard Law alumnus Mo Nooraee are provided for a standard fee of $350 per rug, or donors may supply their own certified third-party appraisal.

Note: Once agreed upon and processed, the donation is legally binding and irrevocable.
