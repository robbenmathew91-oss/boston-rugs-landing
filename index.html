Woven History

Three Centuries of Weaving Artistry

For over three hundred years, the Nooraee family has transmitted the precious heritage of weaving Persian rugs from generation to generation. Today, founder and president Mo Nooraee continues that legacy as one of the world's foremost appraisers and restorers of fine rugs.

Traditional Vegetable Dyes

We respect the pedigree of hand-made rugs woven from natural fibers, handspun wool, and non-synthetic, color-fast dyes.

Museum-Quality Conservation

Himself a master weaver, Mr. Nooraee ensures that every rug is treated with the highest level of care using time-honored methods.

Giving Back

Donating Rugs for Charitable Causes

Service is the foundation of our work ethic. We are proud to facilitate rug donations that benefit local and global communities.

"The Gems and Treasures are Service"

Born into the seventh generation of a family of Persian weavers in Isfahan, Mo Nooraee came under the tutelage of a local master weaver at the age of seven. For his first lesson, instead of seating him at a loom, the teacher had Mo search the workshop for its hidden gems and treasures. Finding nothing, Mo returned discouraged. The master weaver told him: "The gems and treasures are service, service, service, Mo."

Since its establishment in 1979, Noor Oriental Rugs has been committed to community service. We have donated hundreds of thousands of dollars’ worth of woven treasures to charitable causes, including schools, hospitals, research organizations, cultural institutions, and outreach to the homeless.

Free Collection & Pickup

We arrange free pickup in our company van for local donations, and use UPS nationwide for non-local donors.
